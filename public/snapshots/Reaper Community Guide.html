Why play Reaper?

Reaper is your archetypal backstabbing assassin class, with extremely fast-paced movement and mobility unmatched by any other class in Lost Ark. She can be played as a hard hitting sustained burst DPS as Lunar Voice, or as a high APM, high DPS piano gameplay class as Hunger. Keep in mind, you will perpetually chase the boss’s back with your tools as Reaper’s attacks are mostly back attacks. If this is the kind of thrill you seek, then it’s time to pick up the dagger.

What is the identity gimmick for Reaper?

Reaper’s identity gauge can be filled using Green and Purple skills.

Once the Green gauge is fully charged you have 2 options:

  • You can use your identity skill to enter “Persona” mode which applies stealth and creates a clone of yourself.  You can control this clone by hovering your mouse on a location and pressing the “Shift” key. In “Persona” mode you will swap to a Purple gauge that will decay slowly. You will also gain a stacking damage bonus and attack speed up to 5 stacks. This will increase the damage and the attack speed of all Red skills while you are in “Persona” mode. You do not need to fill the Red gauge to enter this mode.
  • You can continue using Green and Purple skills to fill your Red gauge. Once it’s full you will enter “Chaos” mode and gain 10% attack speed, 10% movement speed, and 15% crit rate. The gauge will decay in 9s but can be maintained by using Green and Purple skills.

What are the Pros and Cons of being a Reaper?

Pros

Cons

Great mobility.

Reaper has access to Nightmare and Distortion, which basically lets you dance circles around the boss for your damage uptime and makes getting those positionals a walk in the park.

Extremely squishy.

Even with insane mobility, there are times where you just happen to stand in the intersection of venn diagrams of AoE, and well… pop goes the Reaper.

Decent synergy.

Reaper comes with 12% defense shred, which is a fairly decent synergy compared to others, and also somewhat rare compared to the other synergies in the game.

Positionals are mandatory.

Reaper is an Entropy class and their most damaging skill has a tripod that specifically boosts back attack damage. Your damage will fall off extremely hard if you do not hit back attacks.

Best counter in the game.

Nightmare is a ridiculous counter that has fast startup, can be thrown from range, and the follow-up teleport from behind is also another counter. Using Distortion afterwards also means you have zero downtime on the back.

Requires a decent amount of investment.

Applies more to Lunar Voice than Hunger, but nonetheless she wants a lot of runes from horizontal content or from guardian raids that may not be necessarily available to newer players who have just  recently started playing the game.

Straightforward.

Reaper has an easy to understand playstyle.
Lunar Voice builds gauge with Green and Purple skills,
uses identity with the gauge and does damage with Red skills.
Hunger rotates Red skills with Green and Purple skills.

Mediocre or subpar stagger and destruction.

Reapers don’t have the greatest stagger and destruction in the game. While Hunger fares much better in this department, it still isn’t as good as other classes. They have to make up for their lack of utility with Whirlwind Grenades and Destruction Bombs.

Fun and unique playstyle.

While this may be quite a subjective point, people very much enjoy Reaper’s fast playstyle and their flashy animations. The class is generally well-designed and the rotations are fluid and intuitive.

Tripod reliant.

Reapers very much want tripods to do well, and many of them are high priority targets for upgrades.

Unfairly treated in pugs.

Reapers will have a difficult time finding parties that will accept them, as they have a strong stigma against them for their general squishiness and tendency to die.

What are the key habits of a good Reaper player?

  • Reading up and cultivating good knowledge of basic boss patterns
    in a raid to maintain back attack uptime.
  • Basic understanding of the range of Red skills. They have medium range
    and thus you can afford to damage the boss even if there is a pattern
    that prevents you from being close to the boss.
  • Mastery of the mobility tools at your disposal to keep uptime on bosses and keeping yourself alive. The boss will not always stand still for you, you have to create your own opportunities.
  • Developing a proactive playstyle to take advantage of damage windows by observing boss patterns. Knowing when a boss is going to stand still and get staggered, etc.
  • Greed for DPS within reason.

Lunar Voice really wants a high amount of Specialization to comfortably play the game. Without this, she feels terrible. Hunger is a bit more freeing in this regard, as the stats do not affect the playstyle as much.

Lunar Voice

Written by poyo

Playstyle

Lunar Voice is a gauge builder/spender sustained burst DPS class that takes a decent amount of investment in order to comfortably play. Her goal is to generate gauge with Purple and Green skills for her Persona identity, and use Red skills while in the Persona state. She requires careful deliberation with her damage abilities, as missing a heavy hitting Red skill is catastrophic to her damage output.

While the idea of the gameplay is simplistic, the learning curve is quite high, as getting used to several of her skills and her identity that forces a lot of movement can be quite the daunting challenge. You will need to be dextrous with your hands to control her forced movement in your favor.

70/30 Spec/Crit is currently the most common stat distribution for this engraving.

You can opt for a 70/30 Spec/Swift distribution for increased attack, move speed, and cooldown reduction, but this distribution requires the Earth’s Entropy relic set effect and high level crit rate tripods on your Red skills to make up for the loss of crit rate from accessories.
It is not recommended to start off with Spec/Swift build if you are just starting out.

Lunar Voice

Changes the identity skill to instantly provide a 160% damage bonus and 10% attack speed on all Red skills upon identity activation, instead of stacking damage per second.

Ambush Master

Unconditional 25% damage boost for back attacks. Reaper relies quite heavily on back attacks and this provides a huge damage boost to your skills.

Keen Blunt Weapon

Provides 50% crit damage but has a chance to deal -20% dmg. Requires high level tripods on Red skills to be utilized effectively. With Entropy Lv. 1, you need at least 87.5% Crit Rate for this to give a 16% damage increase, and 90% with Lv. 2.

Grudge

Boosts damage by 20% for 20% more damage taken.
Forces players to play safer due to increased damage taken.

PICK ONE

Cursed Doll

Boosts attack power by 16% in exchange for 25% reduced healing. The damage bonus is not as significant as Grudge but it is also a lot less punishing.

Adrenaline

An extremely efficient engraving at level 1, giving an easy 5% crit and some attack power. Extremely easy to upkeep with just Shadow Vortex. You may consider using this at Lv3 to replace Cursed Doll and have Ether Predator be your Lv1 engraving.

LEVEL 1 OR 2

Adrenaline

An extremely efficient engraving at level 1, giving an easy 5% crit and some attack power. Extremely easy to upkeep with just Shadow Vortex. You may consider using this at Lv3 to replace Cursed Doll and have Ether Predator be your Lv1 engraving.

Ether Predator

Also an extremely efficient engraving, mostly paired with Lv3 Adrenaline to replace Cursed Doll. The Ether Predator set is more self-sufficient and not reliant on crit synergies/high tripod levels compared to the Cursed Doll+Adrenaline build.

Cursed Doll

Boosts attack power by 3% in exchange for 25% reduced healing. The downside of this engraving is pretty horrible for a Lv.1 engraving, but it is a viable option should you not want to put up with Ether Predator as an engraving.

Disrespect

Damage increased by 9% to foes under 30% HP. Another somewhat viable option, giving about 3% DMG on average, but this would mean you would essentially be playing as a 5x3 until this engraving comes into play.

Stabilized Status

Damage increased by 3% when your HP is above 80%. While this is theoretically a better Lv.1 engraving than Cursed Doll, the HP threshold is somewhat annoying to upkeep in raids where chip damage is aplenty.

NOT RECOMMENDED

Barricade

It’s okay as a budget engraving, but your Red skills will be extremely slow because you’re giving up all the Persona attack speed just to make this engraving work by slotting Protection Runes on them, which makes it a really bad choice.

Precise Dagger

Precise Dagger is exceptionally bad for Lunar Voice, as we already have high crit rate with Red skill tripods. You will lose a lot of value from Keen Blunt Weapon and Entropy if you use this.

Raid Captain

Lunar Voice does not gain any damage from Raid Captain, as the movement speed bonus from Persona mode’s stealth dissipates just before the damage of the Red skill is applied.

Mass Increase

While Lunar Voice gains 10% attack speed while in Persona, it is still not a good reason to run Mass Increase as it drastically reduces the speed of your gauge building rotation.

Playstyle Differences

Lunar Voice has three distinct skill sets she can pick from.

Nightdisto

Distortionless

Moonstriker

Info

Nightdisto is the baseline starting point for Lunar Voice, and the build that most people should go for at the start. This build is extremely comfortable as it makes use of two amazing mobility skills,  Nightmare and Distortion, of which Nightdisto is a portmanteau of.

Distortionless is the Goldilocks between Nightdisto and Moonstriker. True to its name, this build drops the gauge deficient Distortion in favor of Black Mist, one of the biggest gauge generators that Lunar Voice has, while retaining the use of Nightmare as a flexible option for mobility.

Moonstriker drops all notions of  utility and mobility and goes all-in on faster gauge generation instead with Call of the Knife and Black Mist. They use 2 or 3 gauge generation abilities before they use their identity gauge to deal their high damaging skills.

Pros

Great mobility.

Nightdisto has access to Nightmare and Distortion, which basically lets you dance circles around the boss for your damage uptime and makes getting those positionals a walk in the park.

Two counters.

Nightmare and Shadow Trap are part of your kit, and can be used for counters.

Not dependent on skill points.

Nightdisto is the build that is least dependent on skill points, as Distortion can be taken down to Lv. 4 without much issue.

Great mobility.

Distortionless retains access to Nightmare, which can be used more freely as it is only mandatory to use it in one cycle for gauge generation as compared to Nightdisto’s two.

Flexible rotation.

Black Mist is able to fill up a full gauge on its own by charging it halfway through the cast, which frees up a lot of room to use your other skills like Nightmare for counters or for mobility.

Decent gauge generation.

Missing crucial gauge generation isn’t as catastrophic, due to plenty of high gauge generation abilities and the use of Black Mist.

Great gauge generation.

Moonstriker generates an absurd amount of gauge in a short amount of time compared
to its counterparts.

Straightforward and forgiving rotation.

Unlike the other builds which can have you hitting up to 5 skills per cycle to fill up your gauge, Moonstriker only needs to hit 2 or 3 skills to do the same.

Highest burst potential.

Moonstriker has the fastest technical burst rotation, clocking at 11s per rotation compared to 15s per rotation of Nightdisto.

Cons

Mobility is inflexible.

Mobility comes with its own set of problems as you are essentially required to use Nightmare to generate a good portion of your gauge.

Slow gauge generation.

Among the three builds, Nightdisto has the slowest gauge generation, and can turn into an absolute disaster when you start missing skills.

Gated by cooldowns.

Due to Persona canceling and increased attack speed during Persona, the rotation can come to a complete standstill as Nightmare’s cooldown is slightly too long, leading to clunkiness.

Awkward movement.

As using Nightmare puts you in front of the boss, you are essentially forced to Shadow Trap, use Persona mode, or walk to return to the back of the boss, which may feel quite awkward.

Black Mist can be hard to use.

As Black Mist’s Fog Explosion tripod makes up a huge chunk of its gauge generation, it is paramount that the boss does not move during the 3 explosion casts at the end.

Skill point dependent.

Distortionless is highly dependent on skill points, and anything less than 408 SP is just not enough to play it to its full potential.

Complete lack of mobility.

Your only source of mobility is the backflip from Persona mode, which may or may not be enough.

Black Mist can be hard to use.

As Black Mist’s Fog Explosion tripod makes up a huge chunk of its gauge generation, it is paramount that the boss does not move during the 3 explosion casts at the end.

Skill point dependent.

Moonstriker is highly dependent on skill points, and anything less than 408 SP is just not enough to play it to its full potential.

Lunar Voice Advanced Mechanics

The Rule of 4

As you look through the different rotations for Lunar Voice, you will notice that the rotations will include
4 Shadow Vortex casts per full rotation of 3 cycles. This is because Dance of Fury, one of our Red skills, has one of the highest cooldowns, sitting at 28s CD. With a Lv. 7 CD Gem, the cooldown sits at 22s. Considering this information in mind, and the fact that a normal Reaper 3 cycle rotation is approximately 15s long, we can derive the following:

Dance of Fury

22s

Rotation Length

-15s

Seconds Left on CD

7s

4 Vortex Casts
(Lv. 5 Swoop Activation)

-7.6s

For Dance of Fury to come off cooldown in a continuous rotation without downtime, you will always be forced to use 4 Shadow Vortex casts in order to get Dance of Fury off cooldown, no matter the speed of your gauge gen. Even with high gauge gen, this will remain a huge bottleneck.

However, there are instances where this rule can be ignored in its entirety.
This only applies to Black Mist-based builds such as Distortionless and especially Moonstriker, which do not always rely on the
4th Shadow Vortex cast in their cycle to fill their gauge completely due to their ridiculously high gauge generation.

This ability to forego the fourth Shadow Vortex helps greatly for short burst windows before downtime where you are not particularly concerned about getting your next Dance of Fury off cooldown for your next rotation, and is the reason why Black Mist-based builds are considered to have the highest potential for burst damage.

Being able to be able to recognize when you can drop your fourth Shadow Vortex is an EXTREMELY IMPORTANT skill, as it can help save you a lot of time from needlessly using Shadow Vortex when your Red skills are already off cooldown. Any sort of brief disengage from the boss of around 2 seconds should already be a cause for consideration for skipping the Shadow Vortex cast.

At the start of the 3rd cycle, if your next Red skill is ≤5s off cooldown: Skip Shadow Vortex

At the start of the 3rd cycle, if your Red skill is ≥7s off cooldown: DO NOT skip Shadow Vortex

Written by Nabzy, Natt

Overview

Abandoning stealth for speed and aggression, this engraving provides players with some of the fastest gameplay Lost Ark has to offer. Skills with fast animations, low cooldowns, and high mobility will allow them to weave  in and out of combat effortlessly while maintaining high damage uptime. Players will prioritize filling their identity gauge so that they can enter their “Chaos” mode. In this mode they will gain 15% crit rate, 10% movement speed, 10% attack speed, and a 12%/18%/25% damage buff. Failure to hit a target with Green skills or cast any Purple skill within 9 seconds will cause the Red gauge to expire and it will need to be refilled.

This build is made for high uptime, consistent DPS utilizing their plethora of mobility skills.

Stat Distribution

Pre-Relic Accessories (Below iLvl 1415)

70/30 Swift/Spec is recommended when initially building your character below relic gear/accessories (pre iLvl 1415). Swiftness is a key part in granting high movement speed, reducing cooldowns and speeding up animations, while Specialization is used to increase your identity gauge generation and damage dealt through your Red skills. You can achieve this stat spread with full swift & and 1 spec ring.

Post-Relic Accessories (iLvl 1415+)

50/50 Spec/Swift is recommended as players obtain Relic gear as they will have higher base Swiftness and Specialization values. Having Specialization to fill your identity gauge to enter “Chaos” mode, as well as maintaining swiftness to keep uptime on the boss make both stats essential to Hunger Reapers. While both of these stats are important, the 50/50 split will not be easily achieved prior to this gear level. Higher Specialization increases identity gauge to more quickly enter “Chaos” mode as well as increases the damage of Red skills.  Generally, aim for around 1050 swiftness and then dump the rest into spec. After bracelet and pet bonuses, this should result in having ~1200 spec. 

60/40 Swift/Spec (1 Spec Earring or 2 Spec Rings), 70/30 Swift/Spec (1 Spec Ring),  and Full Swiftness (with spec on Neck) are alternate considerations for building your character. Swiftness is a key part in granting high movement speed, reducing cooldowns and speeding up animations, while Specialization is used to increase your identity gauge generation and damage dealt through your Red skills. However, high swiftness variants lose a lot of damage in any fight with downtime and is generally not recommended. More info on alternative builds in the FAQ.

Less than 50% swiftness is not recommended due to a loss in fluid rotation.

Hunger FAQ

Is Full Swiftness bad? Why?

The meta 50/50 build already reaches the 140% movement and attack speed cap with a Yearning Support. This means that additional swiftness is only granting cooldown reduction, which reduces the overall value of the stat.

The tradeoff of full swiftness is losing upwards of 20% damage in order to gain approximately 13% cooldown reduction. With higher cooldown reduction, there is more pressure to use skills immediately off cooldown, since not getting your skills on cooldown equates to a direct DPS loss. While this may not sound too bad, most real raids will have forced downtime where the boss is either in a state of damage reduction, invincibility, or straight up missing from the battlefield. In situations like these, full swiftness builds will lose out on more damage than the meta builds
due to losing additional casts.

Even in optimized situations where the boss never leaves, full swiftness only comes out approximately even in damage to the meta build while requiring much more efficient play.  That being said, if a player is often in situations without a Yearning support (bussing or soloing), that may be a use case to invest more into swiftness.

Bracelets - Crit/Spec vs Spec/Swift?

In most builds, the difference is extremely miniscule. Both Crit and Swift are flawed stats in one way or another. As mentioned in the previous section, in most Adrenaline 3 builds Glowing Brand will already overcap crit without a bracelet, meaning additional crit has reduced value.

However, Swiftness is in a similar boat. With Yearning level 3 coming in the future, any amount of Swiftness over 1048 will be overcapping on movement and attack speed and thus only granting cooldown reduction.

Thus, assuming rolls of 100 Crit vs 100 Swift, the comparison is roughly between 2.1% cooldown reduction vs. 3.58% crit rate. Due to the cooldown reduction stat having more conditions for it to be a dps gain (see Full Swiftness FAQ section), Crit ends up being the generally preferred stat even if the gains are miniscule. In the future with 5x3+1 Adrenaline builds, Crit will definitely be better than Swift as Glowing Brand will no longer overcap crit rate even with the bracelet stat.

Crit/Swift bracelets?

With a Crit/Swift bracelet, it is recommended to make up for the lost specialization by using 2 Spec earrings and 2 Swift rings instead of 1 of each. Doing this results in a similar overall stat spread when compared to a standard build using a Crit/Spec bracelet (150/1200/1000).

Is Glowing Brand stronger than Rage Spear? Why are its gems recommended first?

Cast for cast, Rage Spear should be stronger than Glowing Brand. However, Glowing Brand is extremely consistent due to its Crit Rate tripod. Having unlucky runs where Rage Spear doesn’t crit can mean that Glowing Brand ends up doing more overall dps.

On the flipside, Glowing Brand often will overcap crit rate when in a party with a crit synergy, while Rage Spear gains full benefit. In situations like these, it’s more likely that Rage Spear will overtake Glowing Brand as the top damage skill.

For cooldown gems, Glowing Brand is prioritized over Rage Spear because it does not have a Quick Prep tripod. Cooldown gems apply after Quick Prep Tripods, which means that Rage Spear has an effective 16s base cooldown with a Level 5 Quick Prep, while Glowing Brand has a 22s cooldown. The nature of % cooldown reduction from gems means that you get more value out of putting them on longer cooldown skills, which is why Glowing Brand is prioritized.

Spec Pet or Swift Pet?

For all variations of 50/50 builds, spec pet will be the better option as 100 spec is more valuable than 100 swift, especially after already capping attack/move speed. Full swiftness builds get very low value from either pet option (~60 spec vs ~150-160 swift) - either approximately 2% more damage from spec pet or 3.3% more cooldown reduction from swift pet. Either option is acceptable in this case, but once again full swiftness builds are generally not recommended due to their inefficient use of stats.
