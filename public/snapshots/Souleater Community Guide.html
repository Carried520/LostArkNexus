Why should I play this class?

Soul Eater is a class from the Assassin family in Lost Ark. She has two engraving options that each provide unique tradeoffs but offer an overall quick, exciting, and modernized experience.

Soul Eater focuses mainly on a non-directional playstyle, and both builds allow for an extremely high level of flexibility and personal flare, whether you value highly mobile ability spam or quick and soul-crushing burst damage.

How does my identity work?

Soul Eater’s identity is a combined gauge system consisting of two distinct yet seemingly interdependent gauges.

The first identity gauge (Soul Stone Gauge) comprises three (3) Soul Stones and can be filled on each use of basic attack, Scythe skills and Death skills. Reaper skills consume one stone on use and cannot be cast without at least one stone.

The second identity gauge (Possession Gauge) is unsegmented and can be gradually filled with Basic Attacks, Reaper skills, and Death skills. Upon reaching max, Reaper state can be activated and maintained for 13 seconds until the gauge is depleted or until all Soul Stones are consumed.

Upon Reaper state activation,  Damage Amplification is applied to nearby enemies and you’re granted 10% Attack Speed, 20% Move Speed, and +20% Crit Rate for the duration. In this state any Reaper skills used by consuming Soul Stones gain 20% increased damage in addition to damage bonuses from specialization.

Dark Moon

Written by Stein & Sekwah

Dark Moon is a fast-paced combat style that consists of consuming Soul Stones to generate Boundary Gauge and enter Boundary State for 12 seconds.

Detailed Engraving Description

When using the Dark Moon, Scythe skills and Reaper skills gain 20/30/40% increased damage. However, the ability to enter Reaper state is nullified and the Possession Gauge is replaced by the Boundary Gauge, which slowly depletes and can only be increased by casting Reaper skills.

Reaper skills generate 10% more gauge, no longer consume one (1) Soul Stone on use, and can be cast without acquiring at least one (1) Soul Stone but will require three (3) Soul Stones to be enhanced. Enhanced Reaper skills consume three (3) Soul Stones to inflict 25/35/50% more damage and have 30% increased gauge generation.

At max Boundary Gauge, 20% attack speed, 10% movement speed and 300% combat resource recovery is granted in addition to 120% increased damage and 40% cooldown to Scythe skills. However, Reaper skills are disabled.

Playstyle

Although Scythe skills gain a massive boost after being inflicted with Soul Steal, it is important to remember Boundary State is not your main damage window and only serves to keep DPS similar to normal state. Reaper skills have high natural damage, paired with the damage bonus from the engraving, and are still a major part of the damage distribution. Boundary State is merely a tempo change rather than a goal or significant DPS reduction.

Stat Allocation

There’s a lot of viability with different stat spreads for Dark Moon. You can build Full Crit on all accessories, and run Nightmare to help with the long CDs and damage, or opt for more swiftness (in different amounts) and get some Crit Rate from Hallucination. It’s personal preference.

Full Moon

Written by Sekwah, Stein, & Haste

Full Moon is a builder-spender playstyle centered around generating Soul Stones and Possession Gauge at the same time and then entering Reaper state, where your Reaper skills do extra damage and have their cooldown reduced.

Detailed Engraving Description

Full Moon increases Crit Rate by 3/8/14% and recovers the Possession Gauge by 5% when consuming a Soul Stone. When the Possession Gauge is full you can enter Reaper state.

Reaper state grants 10% Attack Speed, 20% Movement Speed, and 20% Crit Chance.

Upon entering Reaper state the cooldown of Reaper skills is reset, their damage increases by 5/10/15% and they have 50/60/70% reduced cooldown.

Playstyle

The goal is to fill your Possession Gauge as fast as possible by using your Death skills and Reaper skills. These skills also generate and consume Soul Stones.

Upon entering Reaper state all three (3) Soul Stones will be automatically generated and the cooldown of all Reaper skills will reset. After consuming three (3) Soul Stones inside of Reaper state, it will automatically end and you will begin the cycle of building Possession Gauge again.

Outside of Reaper state you want to maximize the usage of Soul Stones since Reaper skills are a massive contribution to your overall damage and build up a decent amount of Possession Gauge. The cooldown of your Reaper skills is reset upon entering Reaper state so there is flexibility in their usage and really no benefit to holding on to them for any reason.

         

Stat Allocation

Specialization is the main stat of this build since it increases the damage of Reaper skills.

Secondary stat is Crit Rate to help cover the lack of it.

Rusty Nail vs. Gluttony

The only difference between the 413 and 323 skill setup is the decision to take Rusty Nail or Gluttony as your counter skill. This is mainly a personal preference decision. You may want to take Rusty Nail for additional mobility (i.e. progression raiding) or if you want the ability to change the direction of the counter since it’s a two-part skill. You may want to take Gluttony if you need additional Weak Point or feel like you are lacking in Possession Gauge generation. Many will opt to use both interchangeably as needed so it might be worth it to keep spare gems for each.
