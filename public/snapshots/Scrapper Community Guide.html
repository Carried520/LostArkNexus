General FAQ

This document is a community driven full breakdown of the respective class. This is a live document where we actively change it based on feedback and changes to the game to provide the best information to the community. The majority of us hang around in the Community Lost Ark Discord in our respective class channels. If you would like to give us feedback or ask questions contact us there.

Why should I play this class?

Scrapper does well with minimal investment. The class identity is the simplest out of all the classes and both builds are simple to play, making it one of the best beginner DPS classes to pick up.

Her stagger, destruction, and mobility are all above average as well, with her DPS synergy being the only mediocre aspect. The main rotation for a Scrapper is to apply your synergy and then press your damage skills - there is minimal setup required to output damage for this class. Scrapper is a back attacker class and will require smart positioning on top of playing in melee range of the boss.

How does my identity work?

Her identity consists of two separate bars, Stamina and Shock .

Stamina skills cost stamina, and generate Shock meter. These skills are generally quick and do medium damage.

Shock skills cost shock meter, and generate Stamina. These skills are slow and hard hitting.

How do I decide on a build?

Both have their strengths and weaknesses. It’s a matter of personal preference.

What are my Party Synergies?

Synergies are a buff your party benefits from. These only apply to your party and not the entire raid. Consistent synergies are up for most of the fight while Burst synergies are much more infrequent.

Why is Yearning important?

It’s considered a part of a build despite not being on your character.

Swamp of Yearning is used by supports and gives +8/10/12% Damage and Attack/Move speed to the party. This is a Relic Set they use that has a golden ring.

What’s special about our spacebar?

You have two spacebar dashes. If you use both, you will go on a 10.0s cooldown. If you use only one, you will go on a 5.0s cooldown after 2.0s have elapsed without pressing the spacebar again. You can move in any direction with either spacebar dash.

Shock Training

Written by Volts, Spy

Playstyle

Shock Training Scrapper plays around building Shock Meter while applying synergy, then unleashing slow but powerful shock skills from behind a target for heavy burst damage. There is no real rotation, and the raw defensive stats of this class are the third highest in the game allowing for a simple and forgiving playstyle.

Stat Allocation

Below are recommended stat distributions for the meta builds. It’s recommended to go full spec for the damage it grants to shock skills, with a little bit of crit to make your damage more consistent and to compliment the damage you receive from spec. However, too much crit will result in a lower damage ceiling, and not enough will result in inconsistent damage, both of which will lower your average damage. Typically, only one ring is taken for crit alongside the necklace, but an earring instead of a ring provides similar results before bracelets are available.

FAQ: Shock Training

Questions sourced from frequent interactions within the Unofficial Lost Ark Discord

Can I replace Earth’s Entropy and Ambush Master?

No, the damage loss is too severe to ever truly justify.

How much crit should I build?

Typically, Shock runs one crit ring alongside the necklace’s secondary stat also being crit. However, one can run an earring instead of a ring before having access to bracelets for a similar effect. That being said, bracelets give us more crit, so having a bracelet and crit earring gives too much for it to be as valuable as the spec that we are missing out on. You can also run a full spec build, but this will likely cost more, and is much more inconsistent in the damage it outputs as you are relying on a lower crit chance to output reasonable damage. Due to this, it will also do lower damage on average, and is usually only taken if you are positive that you will consistently be running with several crit synergy classes in your party.

FAQ: Taijutsu

Questions sourced from frequent interactions within the Unofficial Lost Ark Discord

How much damage do I lose if I don’t want to back attack?

For Entropy to beat out Hallucination, you need to back attack more than 49.9% of the time. Average players will have no problem back attacking ~60% of the time. Very good players will get ~80-90% back attacking uptime on most bosses. If you are interested in the math, you can read more in this document here.

What’s the BEST stat spread for Taijutsu?

There’s no “best” answer for this question. In real raid situations, each stat spread performs very similarly. If you want a breakdown of each build, I’ve described each one below:

Full Crit - You would go for this build if you want each hit to matter and don’t like maintaining Adrenaline stacks. You will be significantly slower than the other builds, only slightly faster than a Shock build and dealing less damage than one. However, the lower Swiftness means that keeping your skills all on cooldown isn’t as big of an issue for you. You’ll do your damage rotation and hit extremely hard, then wait for your cooldowns to come back up.

Full Swift - You would go for this build if you are confident in your uptime and want the “highest theoretical” ceiling DPS. Your skills will almost never all be on cooldown, and you’ll always have something to press. The Swiftness stat you went all-in on will ensure you have no issues dodging in and out of attacks, and this playstyle favors those who greed DPS in tight windows where the other builds would have to back off of. The lack of Crit hurts, but crit synergies help out quite a bit. This build is enjoyed by many who swear by its uptime-based playstyle and love zooming around the map. However, forced downtime during untargetable mechanics will mean you are losing DPS every second you are not on the boss, which is fairly common in real fights.

50/50 - You would go for this build if you want aspects of both builds listed above. The most popular build, it has nearly as much Crit Rate as Full Crit, while having a healthy amount of Swiftness. Your skills will be on cooldown for 2s at max, and it rewards players who value quality of life and damage. This playstyle is very balanced, taking the best of both worlds and blending them together. The only drawback will be that since you are not stacking one stat, you will lose some pet bonus efficiency.

Your mindset should be that if all of your skills are on cooldown, you have played your role perfectly. Keep this in mind as you pick a build.

Why pet on Crit instead of Swiftness for the 50/50 build?

It’s just preference. Personally, I run a Legendary pet on Swiftness just so I can use it across my whole roster. It’s just simpler to recommend Crit as it’s a more forgiving playstyle in terms of downtime. Remember, you are trying to get all your skills on cooldown as much as possible - this means that you’ve done all the damage you can, which is a good state to be in.
