General FAQ

This document is a community driven full breakdown of the Paladin class. This is a live document where we actively change it based on feedback and changes to the game to provide the best information to the community. The majority of us hang around in the Community Lost Ark Discord in our respective class channels. If you would like to give us feedback or ask questions contact us there.

Why should I play Paladin?

Paladin is one of the easiest classes to play, cheapest classes to build, and one of the most desired classes for raids. He can perform at a decent/high level without putting in much effort. His naturally high defenses practically make him immortal outside of one shot mechanics. 

How does my identity work? 

Paladin’s Piety Meter

Unlike the other Supports, Paladin only has one orb known as the Piety Meter. Once full, you can activate one of the stances to enable their effects. Landing Holy skills will generate the most amount of Piety, and Punish skills (including auto attack) will generate the least.

 Holy Aura (X)

Paladin’s most important Identity skill. It grants a Damage Buff with a Heal over Time (HoT) and Damage Reduction effect while the Blessed Aura class engraving is equipped. Party members affected by the Holy Aura (12-meter radius) inflict +10% (Base with 0 Spec) Damage to foes. Push Immunity on activation. The efficiency of this Buff scales with the Specialization stat. (45% skill efficiency per 500 Spec, 4.5% additional damage from aura)

 Sacred Executioner (Z)

Only boosts the Range and Damage of your Punish skills. Unfortunately, this is completely useless for Support Paladin. Attack Range of basic attacks and Punish skills +30%. Damage by basic attacks and Punish skills +40%. Push Immunity on activation.

What are the Pros and Cons to Paladin?

Pros

Cons

Cheap

Support is one of the cheapest builds to go in the game since they can sit comfortably at 4x3 and do all the content or invest a bit and refine your build.

Poor Progression

 Past your main few engravings and some tripods your progression falls off a cliff making the increase in power over time feel not very rewarding.

Tanky

Due to never using Grudge, high natural defenses, and a kit with Damage Reduction and Shields, Paladin is one of the tankiest classes in the game. 

Unrewarding

 There is no good feedback for determining how much you are personally impacting the party, except the latest MVP changes and Bible.

Sought After

 You will be instantly accepted to all content and everyone will want you in their party. Support is a heavily demanded role for all raids. 

Blame

Since it is your job to keep everyone alive, sometimes you might get blamed for people dying even when it is not your fault.

Good Damage Reduction

Paladin has a good mix of Damage Reduction and decent Shield abilities in his kit. This can help with occasional chip Damage and heavy hitting moves during the raid. Properly rotated DR and Shields can easily compensate for not having any burst Healing.

Lacks Tenacity

Paladin is often known as a tanky ragdoll due to his lack of Para/Push Immunity on Holy Protection and Attack Power Buffs. This paired with his medium cooldowns could result in Damage loss or less Protection. There’s plenty of Paralysis Immunity on other skills in his kit.

High Sustain Identity

Paladin can build Piety pretty efficiently once you gain the necessary components for endgame and it gets even better with higher optimization. His Holy Aura lasts around 17s and it provides Damage, Heal over Time and Damage Reduction to your party all at the same time.

No burst healing

 Paladin’s only reliable source of Healing comes from Holy Protection’s 8% healing on a 30s base CD. His Holy Aura may Heal the party but it’s usually used as a Damage Buff. The class does not need to sacrifice anything to Heal.

High Attack Power Buff uptime

Paladin has two ~21% Attack Buffs that can be consistently applied to your party. At 1550 swiftness and Lv.7 CD gems, your Attack Power Buff downtime is at most 3-4s. Not to mention, his buffs are “screen wide” so Paladin synergizes well with party comps that are mixed with Back, Front, and Ranged Attackers.

Lower shield uptime

Holy Protection has extremely low uptime of 6-10s (depending on tripod) every ~25s. Godsent Law has a higher uptime and can be rotated with HP to compensate. It offers 70% Damage Reduction which can cover a party member’s mistakes or allow DPS to greed for more Damage.

High mobility

This class naturally reaches Max Movement Speed with Swiftness and Yearning Set. His spacebar dash is around 5s, and he has an option to choose the Charge skill for a second dash, which is not really recommended but can be used.

Awakening must hit to produce Piety

Paladin’s Awakening is the biggest source of Piety generation. It fills around 80% gauge, so missing the enemy causes you to lose Damage and Healing from Holy Aura.

Counter God

Paladin is one of the best Counter classes in the game. Usually two Counter skills are brought to raids and can be saved for Counter/Stagger only. Executor’s Sword has fast animation, a forward dash with Excellent Mobility tripod and a wide arc angle with Broad Slash. Only the first hit counters, second provides Destruction (with tripod). Holy Sword is a very long ranged multihit Counter with a huge Counter window, but slower animation, can be precast.

No ability to generate Piety during Holy Aura

Unlike other Supports, Paladin cannot generate Piety during his Identity Buff which mostly results in lower Identity uptime and slower gameplay. Some meter generating skills (Light of Judgment or Holy Area) do not provide anything except very low Damage, so they are not rotated during aura. It is not a strong con because our standard Atk.Power Buffs still heavily compensate for that, making Paladin overall the most consistent Support in terms of providing Damage.

Very high stagger with VPH

After Stagger buff Paladin became the best Stagger Support and one of the top Stagger classes in the game. Considering skills that contribute the most to a Stagger check and some standard build scenarios where Paladin has both Executor’s Sword and Holy Sword with Overwhelm runes, Paladin is potentially only behind the Destroyer. Remember to never drop HS, ES spot is flexible. Take Dark Grenades. 

Low versatility with skills and builds

Mostly only one or two flexible spots for swapping as well as a small selection of good skills providing decent value. Few almost completely useless abilities that are only usable in Chaos Dungeon or not even. 6 strong core skills (HS, LoJ, HP, WoG, GsL, HB) with two flexible spots and one of them is a Brand where there are only two viable options, either Light Shock or Sword of Justice. Godsent Law should not be used as a Brand, eventually as a second one.

How is Paladin perceived by other players?

Supports are generally seen as a heavily desired role due to their insanely good efficiency for a party, utility and Damage wise. They may be looked down upon for not having to utilize Grudge and they can utilize their naturally high defenses to become the tankiest classes in the game allowing many to “play bad”. However the difference between a good and bad Support is night and day and if you play well you will be praised.

When does Paladin begin to feel good to play?

Due to Paladin being very low investment tripod wise, as long as you have correct stat allocation and the Awakening engraving you are good to go for some pretty effective and fun Support gameplay.

What are the key habits of a good Paladin player?

  1. High Damage Buff (WoG and HB) uptime, precise rotation without overlapping.
  2. High Light’s Vestige (Brand - LS/SoJ) uptime.
  3. Generating as much Holy Aura as possible.
  4. High Shield/DR uptime, rotating HP with GsL.
  5. Saving teammates with GsL during hard hitting patterns.
  6. Coordinating Bursts with your teammates.
  7. Timing Buff windows with Stagger and Boss phases.
  8. Countering.
  9. High Stagger contribution.
  10.   Stimulant and Dark Grenade usage.🐐
  11. Taking on additional roles such as Sidereal usage.

Why is Yearning important?

Every single DPS player will make their build around this set. It is incredibly important for you to focus on getting it to help your team. It is one of the most effective party Damage increases a Support can give and is what makes having a Support more valuable than having a 4th DPS.

2 set Dominion Fang is NOT better than 6 set Yearning. The 6 set unconditional Damage bonus is far stronger than Dominion’s cooldown reduction for permanent buff uptime.

Playstyle

Paladin is a Support that has a more laid-back playstyle and requires less effort to achieve essentially the same results as other Supports. Being a good Paladin is fairly easy due to his simple rotation, screen-wide range for most of his buffs and longer cooldowns that lead to slower gameplay.

It is worth mentioning that there are ways to drastically increase the difficulty, such as

fighting for higher Attack Power Buff and Brand uptime (90/95/99+), greed more Piety for higher Identity uptime (50/60/70+), not holding defensive skills for too long for higher Shield/DR uptime, training your reactions and positioning for consistency with Countering as well as for Godsent Law to be quicker and on point with saving party mates. All of this can be mixed with Magick Stream engraving which fits and works better on Paladin than on other Support classes. This is mainly due to Paladin’s mid-range type of gameplay and the fact that the class does not need to rely on Heavy Armor as much as other Supports (significantly higher base defensive values), Paladins tend to be more flexible when it comes to 5th engraving slot.

Paladin can provide Attack Power Buffs, Brand, Damage Buff, Shields, Damage Reduction, Cleanse, Mana Regen, Movement Speed and should provide extremely high Stagger, Counters. The class cannot burst Heal, it can provide HoTs.

We hope that you will have as much fun with Paladin as we do!

May the light bless you.

Engravings

Here are the Engravings you should aim to use,
and why you would choose specific combinations of Engravings to make a build.

Mandatory

Blessed Aura

With Holy Aura, Damage received -10/15/20% and 2% of Paladin’s Max HP restored every 2.5/2/1.5s for all party members.

  • Your class engraving, activates the Healing and Damage Reduction to Holy Aura.

Awakening

Awakening Skill

Cooldown -10/25/50%,

+1/2/3 maximum uses.

  • This engraving is incredibly important, arguably your most important. Having a lower Awakening cooldown means more Buffs for your party.

Expert

Shielding and Healing effectiveness on all Party Members +6/14/24%. If HP is below 50% additional +3/7/12%.

  • Taken in nearly all builds due to increased Shielding and Healing effectiveness being more valued than other engraving options.

Vital Point Hit

Stagger attack effectiveness +6/18/36%.

  • Makes Paladin one of the best Stagger check classes.
  • Allows your DPS to hold their high Damage skills. Indirectly boosting raid DPS.
  • Stagger check wipe insurance.

Personal Preference

After the core four engravings above, some additional engravings have little impact on a Paladin’s performance. Your personal skill will matter way more. It is hard to go wrong as a Paladin,

so choose any engraving below that suits your playstyle or need.

Magick Stream

Stacks up to 5/10/15% MP Regen every 3s while not attacked. When the maximum stack (5) is reached: skill cooldown -3/6/10%. Being hit removes 1 stack of the effect with a cooldown of 10s.

  • Min-max offensive engraving,

can visibly improve your uptime on everything.

  • Extra Mana Regen.
  • Heavily skill dependent.
  • Harder to maintain in later raids with unavoidable attacks.
  • Still decent value even if played poorly.

Explosive Expert

Bomb/grenade Battle Item carrying limit +1/2/3.

  • Min-max offensive engraving,

extremely good if you manage to utilize additional Darks.

  • Additional costs.
  • Dead engraving if you throw less than 4 grenades.
  • Very useful for longer fights.
  • Works well on any engraving level.

Drops of Ether

Attacks can drop an ether within 8m, cooldown 60/30/10s.

Flash Orb: +15% Crit Rate

Strength Orb: +10% Attack Power

Wind Orb: +10% Move Speed

MP Orb: +10% MP

Defense Orb: +10% Defense

  • Add-on engraving, Strength and Flash orbs increase your party's DPS.
  • Inconsistent and requires teammates to actively grab the ethers.
  • RNG
  • Insanely poor design, orbs spawn under your character’s feet.
  • Minuscule Damage increase even with a good rng.

Max MP Increase

+5/15/30% Max MP.

  • Highest MP cap and Combat MP Recovery.
  • QoL, depending on your playstyle.

Example: Galewind on Holy Protection,

Holy Area build (Mana hungry).

  • Spamming Punish skills for extra Piety from Judgment engraving.

Heavy Armor

All Defense +20/50/100%. This additional Defense is immune to Defense Reduction effects.

  • Almost completely useless when content ends up in farming mode (Old, easy content, speedruns).
  • Slight value gain in fresh hard hitting content.
  • Good for progging on Lv.1 or 2.
  • HA3 is a huge overkill and should only be used in Hell content.

Level 1 or 2

Spirit Absorption

Atk/Move Speed +3/8/15%.

  • Ran ONLY at level 2 for reaching Attack Speed cap since there are not many other useful engravings.
  • Very comfortable at Lv.2 for quicker skill animations.
  • Works extremely well with MS3.

Judgment

Punishment Damage +15/20/25%. When Punishment skill hits, Piety Meter gain +100%. Duration of Sacred Executioner +100/125/150%.

  • Provides min-max Piety gain.
  • The damage boost is useless as Punish skills are saved for utility rather than spammed.
  • Very minuscule gain.

Explosive Expert

Bomb/grenade Battle Item carrying limit +1/2/3.

  • Very good Lv.1 or 2 option to bring some additional consumables in the raid.

Crisis Evasion

When receiving fatal Damage, become invincible for 3s recovering 50% of the Damage taken during invincibility as HP. Cooldown 15/12/9m.

  • A safe option for a Lv.1 to increase survivability.

Not Recommended

Cursed Doll

Atk Power +3/8/16%

Healing -25%, natural recovery excluded.

  • It does NOT increase the effectiveness of your Buffs. Ability Buffs are based on your base Attack Power. This applies to ALL Attack Power engravings, Mass Increase, Adrenaline, etc.

Atk Power -2/4/6%.

  • This debuff does NOT decrease the effectiveness of your Buffs. Feel free to stack it to save gold.

Defense Reduction

Defense -5/10/15%.

  • If you run Heavy Armor this negative is negated and can also be stacked to save gold.

Move Speed Reduction

Move Speed -2/4/6%.

  • At 1570 Swiftness Level 1 has no effect on your Movement Speed.
  • At 1690 Swiftness Level 2 has no effect on your Movement Speed.

FAQ: Blessed Aura

Questions sourced from frequent interactions within the Unofficial Lost Ark Discord #Paladin
This section is ever-growing, and new FAQ submissions are always welcome.

Why is there no DPS section?

Support 2nd engraving is designed more or less to help them with solo content.

At their core they are designed as a Support. A geared out DPS Support will always lose significantly to a traditional DPS class. Some may argue that “well it is in the game so the Devs want us to play DPS Support”, however Support DPS have existed since launch (DPS Bard), and received nearly 0 buffs and always performs worse than a traditional DPS.

It is very obvious of Smilegate’s intention to keep these a Support and it has been stated by them multiple times, they made Supports to be Supports.

Support is a very low supply role that the game is balanced and built around and if you choose to play one it is best for everyone to stay a Support.

Couldn’t I play the “Solo Content” build in raids?

In theory yes but there is a huge chance that no one except your usual static member will take you into their group. The build is mainly for clearing story questlines and the T3 Tower. Especially the T3 tower will need a specific setup since it’s way harder than the previous towers and the loot is definitely worth getting.

How strong/fast is his Chaos Dungeon clear?

With 5x3 or 4x3+, Paladin has one of the fastest clears in the game, Salvation/Nightmare sets help a lot. Even with 3x3 standard build (PS3, J3, C3) Paladin has one of the better Chaos Dungeon experiences in the game.

Conviction and Judgment? C+J? What’s that?

Conviction: Chance to proc on hit with the skill it is attached to and gain a Conviction Buff.

Judgment: Chance to proc on hit with the skill it is attached to and gain a Judgment Buff. Consumes Conviction.

When consumed, gain Combat Resource Recovery by 100% and reduce skill cooldowns by 15% (multiplicative) for 6s. 30s cooldown on Buff ending until Conviction can proc again.

Spec Paladin?

Credit to v#6665 for Pally emotes

Spec Paladin is highly discouraged because Specialization has poor scaling on Paladin. It does increase the Damage Buff on Holy Aura and can slightly build faster Piety (without Awakening), but unlike Bard it does not increase the efficiency of our Heals. This is an issue as Paladin naturally has medium Shield and Damage Reduction uptime thanks to his ridiculously high cooldowns,

Spec makes his overall kit even worse.

Is Hybrid Paladin any good? 
No.

Charge or Light of Judgment (LoJ)?

 Charge is a comfy skill to have because of its mobility, low cooldown and a Shield tripod, it still cannot compete with LoJ though. Ending up as a very situational but decent option for beginners, due to utility differences the better choice is very simple and it is:

  Light of Judgment.

LoJ is currently the best over time Piety meter generator Paladin can have.

↓ more Piety → more  Holy Aura ↓

more Damage, Damage Reduction and Healing for your party.  

The skill has really low cooldown, quick animation with  Righteous Light as well as 

Lv.5  Faith option for more meter. Paired withLegendary Wealth Rune and at least Lv.5/7  it becomes a must have in any Supportive build available. Paladin with proper stat allocation (majority Swiftness) is already fast enough to dodge everything by just running and dashing. It is highly recommended to switch as soon as possible.

The sooner you get used to it, the better.

How long does the Holy Aura last?

Around 17s.

  Which card set should I use?

Early game you should be using 6 set Max HP +12% (4+4+4) cards, there are two options and both are easily obtainable. You’d like to use either Farewell, Weapon or Field Boss II due to good HP increase which slightly improves your Shields and Heals.

During mid/late game it’s preferred to go for You Have A Plan card set which increases personal and party Damage by 1.5% dependable on cards awakening level.

12 piece awakening: 0.5%, 18: 1% and 30:  1.5%

Your long term goals for late game are: 

Master of Spears 30

2.5% Dark Damage for anyone in your party that uses Kazeros’s Legion Commanders.

Lostwind Cliff 30

3.5% Holy Damage for anyone in your party that uses Light of Salvation.  

Currently the best possible card sets to have as a Support.

When should I use Awakening?

Technically you want to Awaken as soon as possible to fill up the meter,

that is the most important goal - Piety, shield is secondary.

Next Awakenings are mostly used off-cd especially when our meter is low,

usage should be delayed in case of high Piety (For example above 50/60%).

 Alithanes’s Judgment should not be used solely in a defensive way and definitely not without hitting the target, it could be considered in some very critical situations but it is highly recommended to protect your teammates with  GsL and  HP instead.

Raids usually have stages with mobs right before the boss encounter so it is good to reconsider building up the meter with standard skills (Though with an early Awakening use and some small delays, like preparing positions in pug, your  AJ should be off-cd for another aura in the right timing but it really depends).


If there are plenty of overgeared players in your raid and you feel like they will clean up the mobs quickly, it is better to smash Awakening straight away or use Stim. If your group is not heavily overgeared use your top Piety generators like  LoJ,  HA,  WoG,  HB  GsL and other lower priority skills to fill the meter faster. It is okay if you will not make it, you should have more time during the first little phase right before the boss swap. Mostly, you should be easily able to fill the meter to full before any G1, to then provide the Identity Buff after the cutscene and still have Awakening ready for another  Holy Aura. Although in some cases you should hold that full Piety for a while, preferably till first boss swap which is pretty quick in Valtan and Vykas, especially if your party is overgeared. You do not want to end up wasting a few seconds of your Damage Buff. Aura holding is situational.

Should I keep Godsent Law only for very dangerous situations?

Definitely no,  GsL has pretty low cd which makes it a perfect defensive skill to rotate with  HP. Paladin is a walking campfire so it is very important to use anything you can to prevent the incoming damage and protect your party. You would really want to use it off-cd most of the time with some slight delays in case of incoming simple patterns or other mechanics.

Goals are to Protect and Prevent - help your DPS greed Damage without the need for dodging and make the bosses look like they do not do any Damage to them, and they actually will not in many situations.

The skill creates a shield (19% of your Max HP with Lv.5 Shield) which does not seem like anything special  but with   Grace (incoming damage -70%) it becomes one of the best skills to cheese any kind of damage in the game. So, with frequent casts and good timings your DPS will not be losing any HP because simple patterns will not be even enough to break the Shield, in some rare cases it will go through but the Damage received should be very low. This is why it is a very good habit and one of the priority things to learn, the only downside is pretty small AoE but with  Wide-Angle Attack (30% AoE radius) it is much easier to land it on all of the party members.

 If one of your teammates seems more addicted to the floor pov than the others, you can always give more attention to that player and babysit.. If there is a possibility, it is good to keep hitting the boss with  GsL for additional Piety, only if you are protecting your Damage dealers at the same time though.

Do people prefer Paladin as a Support over other Supports?

Yes, and no.  

As a Paladin you do not have to choose between Heal or Damage Buff from the Identity,  Holy Aura provides both and even an additional 20% Damage Reduction, all at the same time. In return, Paladin's Identity is slightly weaker in comparison to other Supports, it is mainly due to our uptime on it being lower - we cannot generate Piety when our Buff is active so it takes us longer to generate another Identity. Damage wise, Bard's Serenade is around 5% higher (Only during 16s 3x bar Buff, 12s 2x bar is the same and 8s 1x is 5% weaker), Artist’s Moonfall is the same as our aura (10%) but it lasts for 10s, around 7s less than , Artist uptime is the highest though.

When it comes to standard Attack Power Buffs, our Buffs are considered stronger because of the very good range of 24m on both and not a single one requires DPS players to stand in any limited AoE, making it harder to miss or waste a Buff in case of having a Ranged DPS/Hit Master classes in your team or due to unpredicted boss movement. However, compared to our sisters it is harder to reach 100% Buff uptime but not like it matters much because again, not a single one of our both Attack Power Buffs requires players to position carefully. We end up having plenty of 24s+ 100% Buff uptime windows, eventually even more with high Swift, high gems,  and C+J/QR runes.

In progging, Paladins seem to be overall a bit less desired, especially by casual players. It is mostly because of the HoT type of Healing which is obviously noticeably weaker than burst Healing of Bards and Artists, it is still worth to remember that frequent burst Healing of other Supports is a significant Damage loss resulting in a slower run. Despite weaker Healing, Paladin can still perform extremely well during prog and many experienced players seem to prefer it. Choice is very dependable on the other players' needs and their classes, literally a preference.

In pug, Paladins are considered a very safe, strong and reliable choice due to the fact that the class is generally easier and the skill cap is lower, simply put it’s just harder to misplay but that also depends on the player and build optimization.

We also have Cleanse, Paladin is considered a COUNTER GOD  as well as one of the best Stagger classes in the game and the best Stagger Support. Anyway, all of the Supports are God Tier/S Tier and all of them are very desired for any meaningful content in the game.

I’m struggling to keep track of my teammates' health, any suggestions? What are some good settings to help me as a Support?

There are a few settings you can change in game to help with being a Support.

These are all entirely optional, change them to your liking.

Settings > Gameplay > Name Tags

Here you can disable a ton of the titles/guildes/names people have to make it easier to keep track of specific people.

Settings > Gameplay > Combat Related Settings > Show Damage

Here you can make your Damage font size very small or completely hide it altogether if you want to have the smallest amount of screen clutter.

Settings > Gameplay > Combat Related Settings > Show Combat / Pet

Here you can do things like disable other peoples’ pets and combat effects to make the screen less cluttered for yourself.

Settings > Gameplay > Combat Related Settings > Show HP Bar

This setting can help tracking your teammates' HP Bars.

Settings > Community > Notifications and Interactions > Emoticon Settings > Adjust emoticon bubble size > Minimize

This setting can help with Emote usage overlapping onto your teammates names/health bar.

Settings > Community > Macro Text

Here you can assign a message you want to commonly use for communication, these are unique to each of your characters. Generally only used for Identity and Awakenings due to how long their CD is.

(I suggest having them sent to your party to not spam other members)

Settings > Hotkeys > Skills > Select Dropdown next to Skill

You can now use any of these premade macros on a specific skill.
