Identity: Shield Meter

Defensive Stance (Z)

Continuously drains meter, providing a shield with HP depending on how much shield meter you have. Grants Push Immunity, at the cost of movement speed while active.

Taking damage in this stance drains meter proportional to the amount taken.

The stance will end once the meter is depleted.

 Meter cannot be generated while active.

Battlefield Shield (X)

Consumes all Shield Gauge, moves(ignoring collision) up to 16m to your aim. On landing deals low damage to enemies within 6m.

Shield is obtained in proportion to the consumed Shield Gauge. For 5 seconds, party members within 10m are granted Push Immunity and damage they take is transferred to the Gunlancer.

Once the shield runs out or the 5s duration ends, the energy explodes, dealing medium-high damage to enemies within the area.

25% of blocked damage is returned to Shield Guage.

Cannot be used when shield gauge is less than 20%.

Party Synergies

Synergies are a buff your party benefits from. These only apply to your party and not the entire raid.

This is important in PvE and should always be taken into consideration when planning team compositions for high-end content.

Taunt Cooldown

The Taunt cooldown of a boss is dynamic. The cooldown depends on how many consecutive taunts the boss has received. The taunt does not count if the boss is staggered or if the debuff is still active.

Time between the boss being tauntable:

1st taunt = 20s cooldown.

2nd taunt = 25s cooldown.

3rd taunt = 35s cooldown.

4th taunt = 42s cooldown.

After the 4th taunt the cooldown stops increasing.

Fun fact: Expertise stat increases taunt duration.

Tips and tricks

Turning bosses with taunt

Try to avoid turning a boss around with taunts, as this may grief party members trying to land back attacks. A bad Gunlancer who frequently turns bosses around is VERY noticeable and frustrating to play with.

No shield meter gain while in Defensive Stance

Make sure Defensive Stance is off when using Shout of Hatred, as the shield meter generation is wasted otherwise. The exception is when you are prioritizing taunt/synergy over generation.

Pay attention to teammates

Shielding or cleansing teammates can literally save their lives.

Don't tank mechanics that affect teammates

For example building charm/chaos meter vs Vykas/Kakul Saydon.

Manage shield meter well

Using it to greed damage uptime is good, but doing so too frequently can result in taking unnecessary damage. Learn which patterns are worth tanking and which are not, as you will likely not have the resources to tank everything, even as a Bluelancer.

Push immunity on low Shield Meter

Defensive Stance (Z) can be activated even without any visible shield meter(<1%) to give yourself Push Immunity for a single attack; it won’t prevent taking damage, but will prevent you from being knocked up. If you have a few %, you could also activate Nellasia’s Energy to allow 6s of tanking and Push Immunity, even at low Shield meter, as the Nellasia shield blocks damage before it reaches your identity shield.

The DUF-hop

DUF's first part moves you forward, while the second part pushes you backwards. So if you you tap the skill button(only activating the 1st part), quickly move your mouse behind you and press spacebar then you do a quick little hop jump. The spacebar skips the end of the DUF animation in addition to moving you, making you go faster than you normally would only using DUF.

It’s a bit faster than running normally, but if you’re in Defensive Stance(Z) then it’s much faster.

Identity auto-attack cancel

Turning identity on during the 3rd auto-attack on the first hit will cancel the animation while the last two hits still play out. It has a bit of startup but is faster than letting the animation play out or canceling it with something else that isn't a skill. Turning identity off during the 3rd auto attack on the first hit will do the same, however it is instant and you can immediately auto attack again.
This is essentially useful for two things: The first is that you will move forward instead of recoiling back by canceling the recoil animation, the second is that in PVP it will let you combo auto attacks better.

Key pieces of a Gunlancer build

Gunlancer has a low barrier to entry as it doesn’t require much horizontal content. There are no specific pieces required to make the class function.

Focus Runes start becoming slightly important once you approach endgame though.

Stats

Bluelancer scales very well off Spec stat.

Redlancer relies on Crit stat for damage, but reaches near 100% Crit Rate far below max Crit stat, with the rest going into Swift, so mainly CDR and speed is affected by extra stats, meaning it doesn’t scale quite as hard with stats.

Tripods

Damage of both builds scales hard with tripods. Going from level 4 to level 5 alone can be a 15% dmg boost.

Why is Yearning important?

It’s considered a part of a build despite not being on your character.

Swamp of Yearning is used by supports and gives +8/10/12% Additional Damage and Attack/Move Speed to the party. The Yearning buff is given inside that circular area around them.

Lone Knight (Red)

Playstyle

The general rotation revolves around applying two synergies, activating Defensive Stance and bursting from the front of the boss. Use filler skills in the downtime.

Stat Allocation

Stack Crit, only leaving room for synergies to reach 100%, then dump remaining stats into Swiftness.
As Crit Rate from new sources (higher level entropy set, bracelet, e.t.c…) increases you allocate more stats into Swiftness.

Crit breakpoints 

The minimum recommended Crit Rate is 82%, just where an 18% synergy doesn’t overcap you. You can go above 90%, but it’s generally not recommended, as you’ll overcap with any synergy.

Level 1 entropy: 1370 Crit = 82% / 1593 Crit = 90%

Level 2 entropy: 1314 Crit = 82% / 1537 Crit = 90%

Level 3 entropy: 1258 Crit = 82% / 1481 Crit = 90%

Precise increases crit rate by 3/4/5% depending on rarity.

It’s equivalent to getting a fixed 84/112/140 additional Crit stat that is not affected by pet bonus.

Expose Weakness increases raid wide Crit Rate by 1.8/2.1/2.5% depending on rarity.

It’s equivalent to getting a fixed 50/59/70 additional crit stat that is not affected by pet bonus.

If your party has too much Crit synergy for you (over 100% Crit Rate) swap your pet to Swiftness.

You could also carry an extra accessory to swap in, but that will ofc cost extra Gold/Pheons.

Gem optimization

Damage

Prioritize all lvl7, then just upgrade in the following order:

Surge Cannon = Charged Stinger > Gunlance Shot > Dash Upper Fire > (Counter Gunlance) > (Fire Bullet)

Counter Gunlance and Fire Bullet are the optional skills fighting for your last skill slot. They are optional due to having kinda meh DPS on average, so I wouldn’t recommend going above lvl7 on them. You can even cheap out and leave them at 5.

Cooldown

Lvl7+: Surge Cannon, Charged Stinger, Bash

Lvl7: Gunlance Shot, Nellasia, (Counter Gunlance), (Dash Upper Fire)

Bash, Charged Stinger and Surge Cannon need to be upgraded equally to benefit as they are synced.

DUF Cooldown gem is mainly taken with lvl7 CGL(skipping CGL dmg gem) as the gem doesn’t really do anything aside from giving better counter uptime, unless it’s 2 levels above your main 3 CD gems. But since that’s worse DPS per gold than upgrading the main 3 gems it’s better to leave DUF at lvl7 CD if you run it.

Cooldowns per rotation

Surge Cannon: 30

Charged Stinger: 30

Bash: 30

Gunlance Shot: 36

Shout: 24 (lvl5 CD tripod)

Dash Upper Fire: 20.8 (lvl5 CD tripod)

30s CD with a lvl10 gem is 24s, i.e: skills at ≤24s don’t need a gem.

Gunlance Shot cooldown will drift significantly between rotations, this is why you can’t sustain a 2-use rotation beyond a single rep. Even a higher level cooldown gem won’t help solve that.

Cooldowns per 2 rotations

SC+CS+Bash: 60

Gunlance Shot: 54

Per 2 rotations is relevant because when playing optimally you’re alternating between Max DPS and Perfect Loop rotations. Gunlance Shot is used 3 times per 2 rotations.

Main 3 with lvl10 CD gems are 48s, while Gunlance Shot with lvl6 is 47.5s. So even at lvl6 it can sync perfectly all the way up to lvl10 main gems, but we still recommend lvl7 for more margin to work with.

Things to note about Fire Bullet:
Lucky Chance lv5 proc’d 100% of the time for this pie chart.
Damage variance with Lucky Chance proc can make FB’s DPS go from 2% to 10%. Usually hovered around 5-6% with a mid amount of procs.

In a realistic scenario, you wouldn’t spam FB if it resets a lot and ruins your mana at the cost of not being able to cast your other skills.

If you’re considering running both FB and CGL and ditching nellasia to max out your damage: It’s not worth it. FAQ: Lone Knight

Questions sourced from frequent interactions within the Unofficial Lost Ark Discord #Gunlancer channel

Purple Gunlancer?

Doesn’t exist. Purple is just Red.

Is high/full Swiftness viable?

Yes, but generally not recommended as it will have significant mana problems and will almost always perform worse than the Meta Crit build.

When should I bring Fire Bullet?

You take Fire Bullet in any fights that require destruction with the 211 tripods.

For example: Akkan G2 and a lot of guardian raids.

In other fights, it’s recommended to take Counter Gunlance instead.

I’m low on skill points, what do I do?

If you can’t level all the core DPS skills to lvl12(or as high as they go), level Counter Gunlance down to 7. It’s your lowest DPS skill, so it’s way less important than those extra levels on your core DPS skills.

Can I replace Master Brawler?

No, you already get +20% damage from frontal attacks, and Master Brawler’s +25% increases that multiplicatively to 50% (1.2 * 1.25 = 1.5). This is way too much damage to give up on. (even more damage with 6pc Entropy)

Conviction and Judgement? What’s that?

Conviction - Chance to gain a ‘Conviction’ Buff on hit with the skill it is attached to.

Judgement - When you have ‘Conviction’, chance to gain a ‘Judgement’ Buff on hit with the skill it is attached to.

Judgement buff gives +100% Combat Resource Recovery(Mana) and reduces cooldowns of skills used during the buff by 15% (multiplicative) for 6 seconds.

30 sec cooldown on buff ending until ‘Conviction’ can proc again.

Playstyle

As the tankiest build in the game, you essentially sit yourself in front of the boss, and pummel its face until it stops moving. Simply rotate skills properly for positioning and shield at appropriate times and you’ll die to nothing but mechanics.

Stat Allocation

Specialization increases damage of “Normal”(Blue) skills and shield meter generation.

Spec provides too much damage to skip any source of it. Secondary stat(Necklace and Bracelet) however can be Crit or Swift. Crit offers more burst and around 4% higher DPS, while Swift offers better utility in higher movespeed, faster stagger and higher shield+synergy uptime.

Gem optimization

Damage

Prioritize all lvl7, then just upgrade in the following order:

Thunder > Leap > Shield Bash > Charge > (Shield Shock)

QP+LS Leap deals more DPS than Thunder in Trixion conditions, but it’s usually the reverse in practice as Leap is slower and more likely to miss. ES+L Leap is always less DPS than Thunder.

The Shield Shock skill is optional, but should always be used with a damage gem.

Cooldown

There are 2 routes you can go:

Mostly level10 for max DPS, or Single level10 for max Shout synergy uptime.

Mostly level10

Lvl10: Bash, Thunder, Leap

Lvl7: Shield Bash, Shout

Optional recommendations: Shield Shock or Charge+Nellasia

Bash, Thunder and Leap need to be upgraded equally to benefit as these are synced.

Going this route means a Shout every 3 Bashes, because waiting for Shout cooldown even with a gem makes you lose too much DPS.

Because of that the Shout gem is optional, just strongly recommended as it can help when there's raid downtime.

Single level10

Lvl10: Shout of Hatred

Lvl7: Bash, Thunder, Leap

Lvl5/6/7: Shield Bash

Optional recommendations: Shield Shock or Charge+Nellasia

Cooldowns per rotation

Shout: 24

Bash: 20

Thunder: 20

Leap: 20

Shield Bash: 18

Charge: 16

Shield Shock: 15.6 (lvl5 CD tripod)

20s CD with a lvl10 gem is 16s, i.e: skills at ≤16s don’t need a gem.

As you can see, the only cooldown that will drift between rotations is Shout, this is why you either get that gem to lvl10 with rest 7 or push the other gems to 10 and keep Shout at lvl7.

Shield Bash syncs with just a lvl5 gem all the way up to lvl9 gems on other skills, but for 10 you'll need a lvl6 gem on Shield Bash. The only reason to go higher is quicker re-syncing into rotation after being used outside of it. I don't recommend going above lvl7 CD on Shield Bash.

Shield Shock aligns without a cooldown gem, but the gem helps minimize drift/skips when Shield Shock is used during downtime for Adrenaline upkeep. Similar benefit for Charge with a gem, only that is used for mobility/shield rather than Adrenaline upkeep.

FAQ: Combat Readiness

Questions sourced from frequent interactions within the Unofficial Lost Ark Discord #Gunlancer

Is Swiftness Necklace good?

Yes(for Nightmare), but note that you’re trading damage for utility. Crit will always deal more DPS. The loss is just smaller than most classes looking at that same trade.

Adrenaline vs Precise Dagger?

Swift secondary stat: +20.6% on Adr vs +17% on PD.

Crit secondary stat: +18% on Adr vs +12% on PD.

HOWEVER Adrenaline uptime literally cannot be 100%. “Sweaty” experienced players put Adrenaline 6-stack uptime at ~80% with Shield Shock and ~60% without. These can be considered near-ceiling; you’re unlikely to reach above those numbers in most fights.

80% of the Adr values means +~16.5% with Swift and +14.4% with Crit.

60% uptime would be +~11% with Crit.

However, PD suffers a ~2% loss in value with 10% Crit synergy and 3-4% with 18% synergy.

In short: PD is quite good with Swiftness, but not recommended for Crit as even Hit Master provides 1-2% more damage than PD there.

Hallucination vs Nightmare?

Calc uses Lvl3 set bonuses.

Hallu calc assumes 23% Crit from (643)Crit stat and 15% from Adrenaline.

Assumes +22% Dmg from weapon quality and +12% from Yearning for the NM(Nightmare) Addiction calc(doesn't matter for Boundless).

Hallu: 1.59 = (1+.32)×(1+(0.38+0.28)×(2−1))/(1+0.38×(2−1))

NM: 1.52 = (1+.17)×((1.34+.4)/1.34)

BoundlessNM: 2.05 = (1+.17)/(1-.43)

Hallu is +4.5% vs NM Addiction mode, and NM Boundless is +29% vs Hallu.

Hallu+KBW: 1.87 = (1+.32)×0.98×(1+(0.38+0.28)×(2+0.5−1))/(1+0.38×(2−1))

NM+SS: 1.76 = (1+.17)×((1.34+.4)/1.34)×(1.16)

BoundlessNM+SS: 2.38 = (1+.17)/(1-.43)×(1.16)

Hallu+KBW is +6% damage vs SS+NM Addiction, while SS+NM Boundless is +27% DPS vs Hallucination.

Note that Swiftness secondary stat can make better use of Boundless, almost being able to fully sustain it without a support.

To summarize: Hallucination can be viable even without KBW, but on average it's likely similar to Nightmare. With KBW Hallucination should be slightly ahead of Nightmare.

Performance depends on a lot of variables though.

Elixirs might be what pushes Hallucination more notably ahead in the future.

Quick Prep(2)+Low Shock(1) vs Enhanced Strike(1)+Leap(2)

QP+LS come out to 29% higher DPS than ES+L (3.6x vs 2.8x).

QP+LS is also faster than ES+L(affectionately nicknamed “Fat Leap”).

So why would you ever run ES+L? Because it provides better burst damage, shorter total cast time(same cast time, but 1 cast instead of 2), and also reduces mana drain due to the longer cooldown, which is good for Hallucination builds.

But for Nightmare QP+LS is generally the superior option.

There’s no reason to run ES+LS/QP+L, as these combos mean your Leap no longer syncs properly with your rotation, causing drifting.

Should I use Focus Runes?

For Hallucination: Yes. They are not optional. You will run out of mana.

For Nightmare: Some content benefits from longer burst(staying in Magic Addiction longer) rather than going into Boundless. But that is an unreliable benefit that may even hinder DPS if it keeps you from staying in Boundless for a bit, so other runes are the more reliable way to go.

“Princessmaker”/Staggerlancer build?

While it may work pretty well in extremely niche scenarios like G3 Akkan Lantern god, it’s generally considered griefing in most other content as you’re giving up most of your damage for a tiny increase in synergy uptime and (if the content allows it)1-2 more natural boss staggers.

The increased Shout Synergy uptime is at most 40%(standard uptime of Shout Synergy is 60-70%) of 9% (back/front attackers). This is +3.6% DPS, at best, likely less on average.

This does not come remotely close to compensating for the loss of DPS from running this build. This build will effectively result in around -25% DPS for your party.

Sure, it can help rushing stagger mechanics and pushing some extra natural staggers, but do you really think this is better than +25% DPS for your party?

If you really want to boost team utility you should play Bluelancer with Swiftness on Necklace and Bracelet, Nellasia+DUF and Precise Dagger as this gives up minimal DPS in return for extra utility.

Is Gunlancer a Support Class?

No. While it has many party wide effects similar to supports, Gunlancer does not provide party members with as much damage as supports do, nor can they heal. While their utility vastly outclasses many other DPS classes, a party with a Gunlancer in lieu of a support will feel much worse to play in for party members than one with a real support.
