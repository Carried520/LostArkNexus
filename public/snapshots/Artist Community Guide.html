Why should I play this class?

Artist is a support that enjoys a very dynamic and engaging playstyle. By having nearly every trick up its sleeve and a teammate teleport it ends up having a very satisfying raid experience. Being a good Artist can rely on the player’s skill due to needing to constantly aim many of your buffs and utility at your team. She also has an amazing Identity that allows you to have a very consistent and solid damage buff or an individual heal for a lacking teammate.

ARTIST IS NOT A HYBRID CLASS

ARTIST IS A SUPPORT

How does my identity work?

As you attack you generate Identity, with enough generation you gain a full Harmony Orb, you can use these Orbs on two skills. Your Awakening will refill 2 Orbs.

 

Sunrise (1 Orb)

Places an Orb with a time limit that can be picked up to receive an individual heal, the heal amount is equal to 25% of Artist’s total HP.

 Full Bloom engraving adds: when placed instantly heals everyone within 24m an amount equal to 7/11/15% of Artist’s total HP.

 

Moonfall (2 Orbs)

Costs 2 Orbs, increases damage by 10% for 10 seconds.

The efficiency of this buff scales with the Specialization stat.

What are my Pros and Cons?

Pros

Cons

Cheap

Support is one of the cheapest builds to go in the game since they can sit comfortably at 4x3 and do all the content or invest a bit and refine your build.

Poor Progression

 Past your main few engravings and some tripods your progression falls off a cliff making the increase in power over time feel not very rewarding.

Tanky

Due to never using Grudge and being able to run Heavy Armor, Supports will be tankier than most if not all classes in the game.

Unrewarding

 There is no good feedback for determining how much you are personally impacting the party.

Sought After

 You will be instantly accepted to all content and everyone will want you in their party. Support is a heavily demanded role for all raids.

Blame

Since it is your job to keep everyone alive, sometimes you will get blamed for people dying even when it is not your fault.

Artist Specific Pros and Cons

Tenacious

Many of your skills have great Push Immunity to make them very easy to land.

Bad Damage Reduction

Artists' Damage Reduction skill is very lacking and most tend to decide to not use it.

High Sustain Identity

Artists are great at spamming Identity due to easy generation and small orb usage.

Decide between Heal and Damage

 You have to decide on if you should use your orbs to heal a teammate or if you should do a Damage buff.

Dedicated Cleanse

Very easy to use and stackable dedicated cleanse that allows you to easily cleanse any teammate on demand.

Lower Damage Buff Ceiling

Due to Swiftness stacking and your Identity being a flat bubble amount your biggest Damage buff is lower than a dedicated Bard may be.

Great for Progging

This class is extremely well rounded and can provide everything for a party that may be needed in any raid.

Aiming Heavy

Most of an Artist's skills need to be aimed to be sufficiently used with a team and if you miss your skills or position them poorly your effectiveness suffers.

Playstyle

Artist is a support that enjoys a very dynamic and engaging playstyle. By having nearly every trick up its sleeve and a teammate teleport it ends up having a very satisfying raid experience. Being a good Artist can rely on the player’s skill due to needing to constantly aim many of your buffs and utility at your team. She also has an amazing Identity that allows you to have a very consistent and solid damage buff or an individual heal for a lacking teammate.

Stat Allocation

Artist gets the most value from utilizing Swiftness, the increased CDR is the most effective way to increase your utility, the Attack and Movement speed is a bonus and can help with positioning and casting. Losing some Swiftness for a Spec ring is not detrimental to the class playstyle and performance but is generally not recommended.

Bracelet

Bracelets have a large pool of potential effects and it can get very expensive trying to obtain specific ones. The best way to approach this item is to use one with the correct combat stats for your build and not worry too much about the extra effects until you can afford to spend a significant amount of gold for them.

For combat stats, look for high Swiftness and Specialization, and if possible you should try to find Vitality, Max HP or MP Recovery as an already rolled bonus.


If you decide to start rolling for additional effects, look for any of the following:

  • Dagger
  • Expose Weakness
  • Cheers
  • Enlightenment

Why is there no DPS section?

Support 2nd engraving is designed more or less to help them with solo content. At their core they are designed as a support. A geared out DPS Support will always lose significantly to a DPS player on a traditional DPS class. Some may argue that “well it's in the game so the devs want us to play DPS support”, however Support DPS have existed since launch (DPS Bard), and receive nearly 0 buffs and always perform significantly worse than traditional DPS.

It is very obvious of Smilegate’s intention to keep these a Support.

Support is a very low supply role that the game is balanced and built around and if you choose to play one it is best for everyone to stay a Support.

Couldn’t I play the “Solo Content” Build in Raids?

In theory yes but there is a huge chance that no one except your usual static members will take you into their group. The build is mainly for clearing story quest lines and the T3 Tower. Especially the T3 tower will need a specific setup since it’s way harder than the previous towers and the loot is definitely worth getting.

Can I go 50/50 Spec/Swiftness or full Spec?

No, Artist spec scaling is the worst out of all supports and has relatively clunky skill animations without swiftness. Even with full swiftness some skills have such long animations without Super armor that the min/max endgame build adds Spirit absorption 2 to the build so you can cap attack speed (more about this further down).

What are the optimal engravings?

Due to Support having standard engravings and Artist being one of the squishiest classes, nearly every engraving combination will have Class + Awakening + Expert + Heavy Armor. As a 5th the most common will be Vital Point Hit, this is due to supports generally lacking stagger. Drops of Ether can be enjoyable for some, and Max MP is if you struggle severely with Mana management.

Why is the 5x3+1 going 4x3+2+2?

Due to her highest meter gain Skill having long animation lock and her preferred branding skills not having paralysis immunity while having a long cd getting interrupted/caught in the middle of the animation can happen rather often and holding skills because you need to wait out patterns is suboptimal. Therefore going SA2 can help combat this issue while going from HA3 to HA2 is only a minimal loss.

Why go Spirit Absorption on a Swiftness class?

Due to her highest meter gain Skill having long animation lock and her preferred branding skills not having paralysis immunity while having a long cd getting interrupted/caught in the middle of the animation can happen rather often and holding skills because you need to wait out patterns is suboptimal. Therefore going SA2 can help combat this issue but only at absolute min-max after Ancients gear.

Is the mana hard to maintain and do you run out?

If you are using Focus runes, and mana tripods, you should not struggle with mana. However early on if you are lacking without these Max MP and using your utility more resourcefully may help early on. Generally when following the standard build, especially with mana tripods you should not be struggling too hard with mana but if you are feel free to tweak your build so that you can have a more comfortable raiding experience.

Why don’t you level  Crane and  Tiger to level 10?

There are two main reasons, firstly leveling a skill increases its mana cost to cast, which is worth it if there is a benefit to it. Secondly, level 10 only provides a damage increase which is useless for a support and this also changes the animation of the skill making it more difficult to land and harder to generate meter, especially if you only land a portion of the skill. This is not a problem with the skills at level 4/7.

I’ve heard “Artists are a bad support and even Koreans don’t allow them into their parties”, is that true?

This used to be true to some extent. When Artist was released she was underpowered and it took SG some tweaks/fixes to make the class not useless anymore. Nowadays the decision which supports gets into the raid is mostly depending on preference, some prefer bards, some like paladins but since there is a Support shortage there is a 98% chance that you get in most parties as long you have a decent setup.

So is Artist a good dps? Is she hybrid?

No, the game has a very simple structure, Artist, Bard, and Paladin are Support. Every other class is a DPS, there is no hybrid class that does both DPS and supporting. The supports have solo builds for solo content not legitimately good DPS builds. The DPS classes with some utility only have 1/10th of what a support can provide. There is also a misconception with people thinking that a DPS build with some Support abilities/tripods make sense. A Full DPS Build on a support already only deals a fraction of the dmg a real DPS deals at equal footing. If you run DPS and Support abilities at the same time you are basically a terrible DPS doing 80%+ less than an average DPS, and a terrible support doing 50%+ worse than a normal support.

I’m struggling to keep track of my teammates' health, any suggestions? What are some good settings to help me as a Support?

There are a few settings you can change in game to help with being a support.

These are all entirely optional, change them to your liking.

Settings > Gameplay > Name Tags

Here you can disable a ton of the titles/guildes/names people have to make it easier to keep track of specific people.

Settings > Gameplay > Combat Related Settings > Show Damage

Here you can make your Damage font size very small or completely hide it altogether if you want to have the smallest amount of screen clutter.

Settings > Gameplay > Combat Related Settings > Show Combat / Pet

Here you can do things like disable other peoples’ pets and combat effects to make the screen less cluttered for yourself.

Settings > Gameplay > Combat Related Settings > Show HP Bar

This setting can help tracking your teammates' HP Bars.

Settings > Community > Notifications and Interactions > Emoticon Settings > Adjust emoticon bubble size > Minimize

This setting can help with Emote usage overlapping onto your teammates names/health bar.

Settings > Community > Macro Text

Here you can assign a message you want to commonly use for communication, these are unique to each of your characters. Generally only used for Identity and Awakenings due to how long their CD is.

(I suggest having them sent to your party to not spam other members)

Settings > Hotkeys > Skills > Select Dropdown next to Skill

You can now use any of these premade macros on a specific skill.
